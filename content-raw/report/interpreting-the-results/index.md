## 4 Interpreting the results

### 4.1 Why recent projections show lower levels of future growth

Recent rounds of GLA projections show progressively lower rates of future growth. This follows lower annual growth in recent estimates compared with the rapid growth period from 2004 to 2016.

Short-trend variants are most affected by recent lower growth. In the current release, the 5-year trend variant is based on years ending mid-2018 to mid-2022, when London grew much more slowly than in earlier periods.

The latest outputs also project fewer births and more deaths than previous rounds. Lower births reflect continued observed declines and methodological updates to fertility assumptions. Higher deaths reflect revised mortality calibration from recent observed data.

### 4.2 Reasons for lower growth since 2016

Key drivers include:
- Falling fertility rates across the UK and in London.
- Lower net international migration after 2016.
- A recovery in net domestic outflows to the rest of the UK.
- Significant COVID-19 effects on both mortality and migration.

Although London returned to growth in the year to mid-2022, long-term post-pandemic impacts remain uncertain.
