## 3 Components of change

Births, deaths and migration all contribute to London's changing population. Natural change, the difference between births and deaths, is historically a major direct contributor to growth because London has a comparatively young age profile.

As London's population ages, annual deaths are projected to rise while births remain below historical peaks. The balance between natural change and migration drives differences between variants.

Total net migration is the sum of all flows in and out of an area. Historically, London has had high turnover with comparatively small net balances. COVID-19 disrupted both domestic and international migration patterns, including unusually high net outflows in 2021.
